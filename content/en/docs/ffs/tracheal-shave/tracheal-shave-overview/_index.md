A tracheal shave ({{< wiki Chondrolaryngoplasty en >}}) removes part of the thyroid cartilage to reduce the prominence of the Adam's apple, which may help feminize appearance for MtF people. Results vary. Some people can achieve a nearly flat contour, while others may still have a small prominence. Usually only the projecting tip can be reduced; cartilage below the tip cannot be completely removed.

## Prerequisites {#prerequirements}

- Some medical institutions may require a diagnosis letter or referral.

## Consultation {#outpatient}

Tell the doctor that you want the procedure and receive an initial assessment. Some people are not suitable candidates or may be advised against surgery if the expected effect is minimal.

## Surgical Process {#workflow}

The procedure involves anesthesia, cartilage reduction, and suturing. The eyes may be covered during surgery.

If you need to swallow or vocalize during cartilage reduction, tell the doctor beforehand and only do so with permission.

## Aftercare {#after-care}

Use cold compresses after surgery, avoid swallowing and speaking as much as possible, and avoid eating or drinking for several hours if instructed.

Discuss pain relief with the doctor if needed.

Scar care products, such as scar gel or silicone sheets, may be used according to medical advice to reduce scarring.

## Voice Training {#therapy}

In theory, tracheal shave does not affect voice production. If you plan to do [voice feminization training]({{< ref "vocal-therapy-overview" >}}) after surgery, tell the trainer about the procedure in advance.
