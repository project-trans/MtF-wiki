## Overview

It is estimated that many people assigned male at birth develop some degree of hair loss as they age. Unfortunately, most hair loss is not easy to reverse, so prevention is often the best treatment.

The typical androgenized hairline may be about 0.5-1.5 cm higher than a typical feminine hairline, and androgenetic alopecia can make this more pronounced.

## Non-Surgical Treatment

### Hormone Therapy

In general, trans women who are already on gender-affirming hormone therapy, especially those whose testosterone is already sufficiently low, may not need additional medicines such as finasteride. Finasteride works by reducing conversion of T (testosterone) to DHT (dihydrotestosterone), thereby reducing DHT-related hair loss. If there is little testosterone available for conversion, the benefit may be limited. Some doctors may still include finasteride as part of treatment.

### Laser

Various laser treatments claim to stimulate hair regrowth by using light to increase blood flow to dormant follicles. The evidence and suitable use cases are difficult to assess, and these treatments are not broadly used as a standard approach.

### Minoxidil

Minoxidil is one of the oldest and best-known pharmacologic treatments for hair loss.

Minoxidil, sold under brand names such as Rogaine, has an incompletely understood mechanism. It is thought to increase blood flow to hair follicles, trigger the growth phase, and prolong it.

Common options include topical foam and spray. Minoxidil was mainly studied for hair loss on the crown, but newer evidence suggests it may work on other scalp areas as well.

Topical minoxidil is usually well tolerated, but common side effects include itchy eyes, itching, redness, or irritation at the treated area, and unwanted hair growth elsewhere. Temporary shedding is also common. Minoxidil is suspected to be highly toxic to cats, so do not let cats lick your scalp.

## Surgical Treatment

The most common method is follicular unit transplantation (FUT), which takes follicles from the occipital area, where hair is relatively resistant to DHT, and places them in balding areas. Pricing usually depends on the number of follicles transplanted.

If hair loss continues after transplantation, the transplanted hair may remain while surrounding areas keep thinning.
