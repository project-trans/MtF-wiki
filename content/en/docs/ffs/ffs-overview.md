## What is FFS? {#what}

FFS means facial feminization surgery ({{< wiki "Facial Feminization Surgery" en >}}).

After androgen-driven puberty, a face may have features such as a prominent brow, sharper facial angles, a higher or wider nose, wider cheekbones, a broader jaw or chin, a longer philtrum, downturned mouth corners, or facial hair. A surgeon can make an individualized plan to reduce features that feel masculinizing and support a more feminine appearance.

## Why FFS? {#why}

FFS is not required; it is part of cosmetic and reconstructive surgery. Before pursuing it, clarify which areas you want to change and what outcome you expect. Drawing, makeup, hairstyle changes, or trying different presentation styles can help make your goals more concrete.

Some doctors may not know the abbreviation FFS, especially in regions where transgender healthcare is less developed. It can be more effective to describe the specific concerns, such as brow, nose, jaw, cheekbones, chin, or facial hair.

FFS does not guarantee that someone will look “better.” **Feminization is not the same as beautification.** Hair, clothing, makeup, and changes from hormone therapy may improve appearance without surgery, so compare options before making irreversible decisions.

For larger procedures such as bone contouring, discuss the options carefully with the surgeon and people who support you. Some areas, especially cheekbones and brow-related soft tissue, may continue to change with hormone therapy or fat redistribution. It may also be worth asking whether forehead fat grafting could meet your goals instead of brow bone surgery.

## Aesthetic Medicine {#beauty}

Facilities that provide aesthetic medicine may offer laser hair removal, intense pulsed light, scar treatment, mole removal, and similar services. Availability and suitability vary by clinic.
