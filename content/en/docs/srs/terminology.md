“Genital surgeries” include, but are not limited to, sex reassignment surgery, gonadectomy, partial or complete removal of genital tissue, vaginoplasty, and other transgender-related surgeries involving the genital area.

Gender-affirming surgery, also called sex reassignment surgery (SRS), is often used colloquially to refer to transgender-related genital surgery.

The broader concept of gender-affirming surgery may also include, for transfeminine people, facial feminization surgery including tracheal shave or voice surgery, breast augmentation, orchiectomy, vaginoplasty or vulvoplasty with or without depth, cosmetic surgery, and preoperative preparation such as hair removal.[^2][^3]

For easier categorization in this wiki, gender-affirming surgeries are divided by body region. The “Genital Surgeries” section specifically records transgender-related genital surgeries and related information.

[^3]: Standards of Care for the Health of Transgender and Gender Diverse People, Version 8, Appendix E: Gender-Affirming Surgical Interventions <https://project-trans.org/SOC-8/soc8cn/appendixE>
