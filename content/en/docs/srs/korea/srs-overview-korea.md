## Overview

South Korea is widely regarded as one of Asia's surgically advanced destinations for gender-affirming care. The first known MtF SRS in Korea was performed by Professor [Kim Seok-Kwun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kim_Seok-Kwun) at Dong-A University Hospital in 1986; he is reported to have performed over 320 such surgeries to date.[^kim-wiki][^transhealthcare] However, **gender-affirming care is not covered by the National Health Insurance Service (NHIS)** in Korea — patients pay out of pocket for HRT, SRS, FFS and related procedures.[^lgbt-health][^kihasa]

## Legal Gender Recognition {#legal}

South Korea has no statute on legal gender recognition. The Supreme Court's **Family Relations Registration Directive No. 550** ("Guidelines for handling gender-correction permission cases for transgender persons")[^예규] serves as the courts' working reference. Until recently it effectively required external genital reconstruction and sterilization; the **February 2024 revision** reclassified these from "approval criteria" to "reference points".[^lawtimes-2024][^khan-2025]

Key recent developments:

- **24 Nov 2022 Supreme Court (en banc) decision** — permitted legal gender change for a (non-married) trans woman with minor children, partially overturning the 2011 precedent.[^supreme-2022][^amnesty-2022]
- **2024 onward, multiple lower-court rulings** — granted legal gender change to applicants who had not undergone genital reconstruction.[^mt-2024][^khan-2025]
- **2023 National Human Rights Commission of Korea** — recommended the Supreme Court revise the directive's surgery requirement clauses as human-rights-violating.[^nhrck]

Outcomes still vary heavily by judge; applicants should consult a lawyer or organizations such as **SOGI Law & Policy Research Group** before filing.

## Military Service {#military}

Korea has mandatory male conscription. Trans women still registered as male in the Family Relations Register are subject to military examination.

- Those who have completed external genital reconstruction **and** completed legal gender correction are exempt from the conscription examination.
- Those who have completed surgery but not legal correction may receive Grade 5 (wartime reservist) status and be effectively exempt.
- Under 2024 revisions to the *Rules for Military Physical Examinations*, **at least 6 months of hormone therapy** is treated as objective evidence of social gender identity.[^scmp-2024][^kt-2024]

## Foreigners {#foreigners}

- **Surgery access**: Foreigners may receive SRS in Korean hospitals. Several university hospitals (e.g., Korea University Anam) and select private clinics operate international patient centers and offer English support.[^medical-avenue]
- **Legal gender correction**: Korean Family Relations Registration applies only to Korean nationals. Foreign patients should arrange notarization / apostille of Korean medical records and surgical certificates for use in their home country.

## Surgical Techniques {#tech}

Common techniques offered by Korean providers:

- **Penile Inversion (PI)** — the most common technique.
- **PI + Scrotal Flap** — used to gain additional depth.
- **Sigmoid Colon Vaginoplasty** — typically performed at large multidisciplinary hospitals such as Kangdong Sacred Heart.
- **Robotic Peritoneal Vaginoplasty** — newer technique adopted by some university hospitals.

## Cost {#fee}

As all procedures are out-of-pocket, totals vary substantially. KIHASA and other sources report typical MtF SRS packages of **₩15–40 million**, exclusive of psychiatric evaluation, screening tests, hospitalization, and dilation supplies.[^kihasa][^lgbt-health]

## Post-operative

- Korean providers typically schedule short-interval follow-ups; plan to remain in Korea for **2–4 weeks** post-discharge.
- Twice-daily dilation is the norm for the first months.
- Before leaving, request English-language operative reports, discharge summaries, and dilation protocols — these are essential for legal procedures and follow-up abroad.
