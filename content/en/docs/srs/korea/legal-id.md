## Overview

South Korea has no statute on legal gender recognition. Applicants must petition a **Family Court** (or the registry-competent district court) for *"gender correction permission"* under the Supreme Court's **Family Relations Registration Directive No. 550**.[^예규][^lawtimes-2024]

{{< notice warning >}}
This page is informational. Outcomes still depend significantly on the assigned judge. For an actual filing, consult a lawyer or one of the Korean organizations listed below.
{{< /notice >}}

## Reference Requirements

- **Age** — 19 or older.
- **Marriage** — not currently married.
- **Children** — having minor children is no longer an absolute bar (2022 Supreme Court en banc decision).[^supreme-2022]
- **External genital reconstruction** — reclassified from criterion to **reference point** in February 2024.[^lawtimes-2024][^khan-2025]
- **Sterilization** — likewise reclassified, though some courts still consider it in practice.
- **Psychiatric diagnosis** — F64.0 Gender Identity Disorder (or ICD-11 HA60) certificate.
- **HRT history** — typically at least one year.

## Typical Process

1. **Gather documents** — family relations certificate, basic certificate, resident registration extract, psychiatric diagnosis, HRT records, surgical records (if any), personal statement.
2. **Petition** — file with the family court of your residential jurisdiction.
3. **Hearing** — non-public examination. Some judges additionally require statements from family members or co-workers.
4. **Decision** — once granted, register the correction at the city/ward office within one month. Subsequent changes to the resident registration number suffix, driver's licence, passport, and health insurance follow.

## Foreigners

The Korean Family Relations Registration system applies only to Korean nationals. Foreigners' residence cards reflect the gender shown on their home-country documents. If you intend to use Korean medical records for gender correction in your home country, arrange **notarization and an apostille** in advance.

## Military Service

Before legal correction, applicants registered as male are subject to military examination. Completion of external genital reconstruction with completed legal correction exempts from the examination; surgery alone (without legal correction) typically results in a Grade 5 (wartime reservist) classification.[^scmp-2024] Plan around possible conscription notices while your case is pending — coordinate with your lawyer.
