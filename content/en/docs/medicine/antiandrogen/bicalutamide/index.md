Bicalutamide is a nonsteroidal antiandrogen that acts on androgen receptors and blocks androgen binding. Its antagonistic effect is much stronger than spironolactone, so lower doses can be used. It is widely used for prostate cancer and has recently been used in GAHT, but the medical community remains cautious about this newer use[^3]. It is usually much more expensive than conventional options such as cyproterone acetate and spironolactone.

## Precautions

- According to the current *Standards of Care for the Health of Transgender and Gender Diverse People, Version 8* (SOC 8), **bicalutamide is not currently recommended for routine GAHT** because MtF-related evidence remains limited[^6].
- Use cautiously in patients with moderate or severe hepatic impairment[^2].
- Check serum transaminases before starting and monitor regularly during the first 6 months of treatment[^5].
- Do not use in people with hereditary galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.
- Do not combine with terfenadine, astemizole, or cisapride.

## Route and Dosage

Oral, 25-50 mg daily[^1].

## Side Effects

- **Severe liver injury**[^3] [^5] {{< tag/neg "Stop immediately if detected" >}}
  - Usually occurs within the first 6 months. It mainly presents as elevated liver transaminases and can cause liver failure or death in severe cases[^3].
  - Other signs include nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, fatigue, loss of appetite, flu-like symptoms (fever), dark urine, and jaundice (yellowing skin or eyes)[^5] [^2].
  - Death from severe bicalutamide-induced hepatotoxicity has been reported in an MtF patient[^3].
- **Pulmonary toxicity**: extremely rare, but may progress to severe conditions such as interstitial pneumonia or eosinophilic pneumonia[^2] [^3] [^5].
  - This risk appears much higher in Asian populations than in other populations[^5].
  - Related signs include wheezing or shortness of breath, cough, and pharyngitis, usually presenting as sore throat[^5].
- **Other symptoms**: diarrhea, constipation, dizziness, dry skin, itching, rash, fatigue, edema, changes in photosensitivity, hematuria, frequent urination, and anemia related to renal failure[^4] [^2].
