Spironolactone is a potassium-sparing diuretic and specific aldosterone antagonist used for heart failure, hypokalemia, hypertension, and other conditions[^3]. It also blocks androgen receptors and therefore has antiandrogenic properties. However, spironolactone itself does not lower testosterone, its blocking potency is weak, and relatively high doses are needed for a sufficient effect[^1] [^4].

For MtF people, when spironolactone is combined with moderate-dose estradiol by non-injection routes, testosterone often decreases substantially but may remain above the typical female range. Cisgender women treated for androgen-related symptoms usually use 50-200 mg/day, while MtF people may require higher doses, such as 100-300 mg/day or even 400 mg/day, to block remaining androgens[^1] [^5]. Side effects such as frequent urination and hyperkalemia may become more severe.

## Precautions

{{< tag/neg "Contraindication" >}} **Do not use in people with hyperkalemia.**[^3]

- Before starting spironolactone, potassium should be below 5.0 mmol/L and kidney function should be normal. Monitor potassium, creatinine, and uric acid regularly during use[^1] [^3].
- Use cautiously in people with impaired liver function, especially cirrhosis, because spironolactone may affect the nervous system and cause coma[^3].
- Use cautiously with lithium-containing medications such as lithium carbonate; monitor serum lithium regularly[^3].
- Avoid excessive intake of potassium-rich foods, such as bananas, kelp, and potassium-containing salt substitutes[^1] [^3].
- Do not combine with other potassium-sparing medications[^1] [^3].
- Drink enough water.

## Route and Dosage[^1] [^5]

Oral. 100-400 mg daily, divided into multiple doses. **Some tablets have an unpleasant bitter taste.**

## Side Effects[^1] [^2] [^3]

{{< notice warning "Note" >}}
Only some more noticeable side effects are listed here. See the package insert for details.
{{< /notice >}}

- **Hyperkalemia**: the most serious side effect of spironolactone; it can lead to hospitalization or death.
- **Frequent urination**
- Low blood pressure or low blood volume
- **Hyponatremia**
- Dizziness
- Fatigue and drowsiness
- Dry skin and rash
- Ataxia
- Increased cortisol levels
- Gastrointestinal reactions, including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and gastritis
- Hypersensitivity reactions

People with risk factors such as older age (over 45), kidney dysfunction, combined use with other potassium-sparing medications, or potassium supplements should use spironolactone cautiously and monitor potassium when necessary. Use caution with medications that may affect sodium, such as tricyclic and SSRI antidepressants; sodium monitoring may be needed.
