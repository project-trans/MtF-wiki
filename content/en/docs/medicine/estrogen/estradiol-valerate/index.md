Estradiol valerate is a synthetic, chemically modified estrogen made by esterifying estradiol with valeric acid. After entering the body, it is rapidly hydrolyzed by esterases into estradiol and produces estrogenic activity.

## Route and dosage

- Route: oral. Sublingual/buccal use is also possible and may absorb better, but the evidence remains limited[^1] [^3]
- Dosage: 2-6 mg/day
- Absorption: according to the package leaflet, a single 1 mg Progynova dose provides about a 15 pg/mL **peak** serum level, and Climen 2 mg reaches about 30 pg/mL. Both are far below 100 pg/mL. Bioavailability varies between individuals.

## Side effects

- **Venous thromboembolism**; coagulation can be checked during routine health examinations[^4]
- Nausea, abdominal pain, bloating, and other gastrointestinal symptoms
- Breast tenderness
- Weight changes
- Reduced sexual function and infertility
- Increased breast cancer risk. Screening should be considered after long-term use, for example after age 50[^2]

{{< notice warning "Warning" >}}
Overdose may make these side effects more obvious. Monitor hormone levels regularly and adjust the dose as needed.
{{< /notice >}}

## Ingredient information

- Active ingredient: estradiol valerate ({{< wiki "Estradiol valerate" en >}})
- Bioavailability: oral 3-5%; sublingual 6-25% (estimated)[^3]
- Half-life, similar to estradiol: oral 12-20 hours; sublingual 8-18 hours
- Molecular formula: C<sub>23</sub>H<sub>32</sub>O<sub>3</sub>
- Molecular weight: 356.50
