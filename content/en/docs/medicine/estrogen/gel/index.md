Read the package leaflet carefully and use medicines under medical supervision.
{{< /notice >}}

Estradiol gel is absorbed through the skin. It is convenient and fully avoids hepatic first-pass metabolism, so it is generally safer than oral routes. Overall cost is higher.

Expert consensus: [雌二醇凝胶临床应用指导建议](http://medi-guide.meditool.cn/ymtpdf/E60E60E5-CF7F-0A27-75B3-BE7B935D762D.pdf)[^4]

## Use and dosage {#usage}

{{< notice info "Guide" >}}
{{< expand "Oestrogel dosage card" >}}
![Oestrogel dosage card](/images/medicine/gel/estradiol-gel-intro.jpg)
{{< /expand >}}
Video: [爱思妥用药指导](https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV1f44y1D7To)
{{< /notice >}}

- **Timing:** morning or evening use is recommended[^5].
- **Route: topical skin use.** Apply to areas that absorb well and do not interfere with daily activity, such as the arms, abdomen, buttocks, or inner thighs[^1]. Absorption from these sites is broadly similar[^12]. Rubbing is unnecessary, and the medicine does not stain clothing after it dries.
- **Dose:** 1-2 measuring rulers per day (2-4 pumps), equivalent to 2.5-5 g gel[^5]. With 1 measuring ruler (2.5 g) daily, mean steady-state plasma estradiol is 60-80 pg/mL[^1] [^5] [^7].
- **Dispensing:** make sure the tube opening is fully open, then slowly squeeze from one end of the ruler groove to the other so the groove is fully filled with a rounded cylinder of gel.

**Notes:**

- **Do not apply to mucous membranes.** Alcohol may irritate mucosa.
- **Do not apply around the breasts.** {{< tag/neg "May induce breast cancer!" >}} [^1] [^2] [^5]
- The gel contains alcohol (ethanol). Avoid broken skin and keep away from open flames until dry[^2].
- Wait until the gel dries before dressing[^2].
- Avoid water, sunscreen, and cosmetics for at least one hour after application, because they may affect absorption[^2] [^6].
- Applying gel near a blood-draw site, such as the upper arm, may make estradiol results falsely high.
- If the effect seems too strong or too weak, consult a doctor to adjust the dose.

## Side effects[^1] [^2] [^7]

- **Venous thromboembolism** {{< tag/pos "Much lower risk than oral tablets" >}}[^3]
- Nausea, diarrhea, abdominal pain, bloating, and other gastrointestinal symptoms
- Rash, itching, and application-site reactions
- Breast tenderness
- Weight changes
- Reduced sexual function and infertility
- Increased breast cancer risk
- Flu-like symptoms, such as fever

{{< notice warning "Warning" >}}
Overdose may make these side effects more obvious. Monitor hormone levels regularly and adjust the dose as needed.
{{< /notice >}}
