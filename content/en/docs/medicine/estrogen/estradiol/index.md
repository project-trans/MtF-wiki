Estradiol tablets are usually estradiol hemihydrate, which is different from [estradiol valerate]({{< ref estradiol-valerate >}}). Their potency is equivalent to endogenous estradiol.

### Notes

- Dydrogesterone in some Femoston<sup>&copy;</sup> tablets is an oral medication used in feminizing hormone therapy[^1]. It is a progestin, but it may not provide full progestogenic efficacy[^2] [^4], has no anti-androgenic effect[^3], and has uncertain risks. Use with caution.

## Adverse effects

Common adverse effects include depression, headache, abdominal pain/nausea, edema, and weight gain[^8]. See [medication risks]({{< ref "risk" >}}) for details.

## Route and dosage

- Route: oral or sublingual; sublingual tablets feel slightly dry and have no strong taste
- Dosage: 2-6 mg/day[^5]
- For sublingual use, if taking more than 1 tablet/day, split the dose and take 1 tablet at a time. The total daily dose may be slightly lower than with oral swallowing[^5] [^7]

## Ingredient information

- Active ingredient: {{< wiki 雌二醇 >}} ({{< wiki Estradiol en >}})
- Half-life: 13-20 hours when swallowed; 8-18 hours sublingually[^6]
- Molecular formula: C<sub>18</sub>H<sub>24</sub>O<sub>2</sub>
- Molecular weight: 272.38
