{{< notice warning "Medication safety warning" >}}
Estradiol patches differ from other medicines, and route and dosage should be determined by a doctor according to individual circumstances. Read the leaflet carefully and use medicines under medical supervision.
{{< /notice >}}

Estradiol patches are absorbed through the skin. They are convenient and fully avoid hepatic first-pass metabolism, so they are generally safer than oral routes. They are usually more expensive than other routes.

## Use and dosage

**Follow medical advice or the product leaflet.** In general, use two patches per week (Estrana<sup>&reg;</sup> Tapes: one every two days; Climara<sup>&reg;</sup>: one weekly), applied to the inner thigh, buttock, or abdomen. Swimming and bathing are usually possible without removing the patch.\
The theoretical effects above come from product leaflets and are for reference only. Effects vary between individuals, and occasional reports show very high levels from one patch. **Use actual blood-test results as the basis for dosing.** A 100 µg/day patch, combined with endogenous estradiol, may reach the lower end of the [recommended HRT range]({{< ref "monitoring" >}}), around 100 pg/mL. Adjust dose to the individual situation and avoid unnecessary risks.

Additional notes:

- {{< tag/neg "Contraindication" >}} **Do not use if you have breast cancer, thrombosis, or a history of either.**
- Some products contain ethanol. Do not place them on broken skin.
- If a patch is used near the blood-draw site, such as on the upper arm, estradiol lab results may be falsely high.
- Avoid direct sunlight on the patch.
- Rotate application sites and leave more than one month before reusing the same site.
- **Do not place on the breasts.** {{< tag/neg "May induce breast cancer!" >}}
- See the [patient section of the Estraderm leaflet](https://tfsci.mtf.wiki/misc/estraderm/#how-and-where-to-apply) for more information.

## Side effects

{{< notice warning "Warning" >}}
This section lists only some common or serious side effects. See the product leaflet for details.\
Overdose may make these effects more obvious. Monitor hormone levels regularly and adjust the dose as needed.
{{< /notice >}}

- **Redness, swelling, or irritation at the application site**
- **Venous thromboembolism** {{< tag/pos "Risk is much lower than with oral tablets" >}}
- Nausea, abdominal pain, bloating, and other gastrointestinal symptoms
- Breast tenderness or tension
- Weight changes
- Changes in libido
- Increased breast cancer risk
- Dermatitis, rash, itching
- Depression, agitation
- Headache, insomnia

## Ingredient information

- Active ingredient: {{< wiki 雌二醇 >}} ({{< wiki Estradiol en >}})\
  Some products contain estradiol hemihydrate
- Half-life: while a patch is in use, estradiol is delivered at a constant rate, so a half-life cannot be measured. After patch removal, the half-life is about 1-2 hours
- Molecular formula: C<sub>18</sub>H<sub>24</sub>O<sub>2</sub>
- Molecular weight: 272.38
